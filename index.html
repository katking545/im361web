How to prepare for your audition

Whether you’ve been playing for one year or ten, we can all get some nerves when its audition time. While a little nervousness is healthy, you don’t want to be completely nervous! One of the best ways to help with nerves is to be prepared. Heres an example of how to set yourself up for success.

One Month Before

This is when we want to be finished learning our solos and scales. Here we’ve set up a sample practice session.

30 min. Scales

20 min. Etudes

45 min. Solo

One Week Before

When you get this close to the audition, you want to make sure your solo is fresh in your mind, but make sure you aren't overworking yourself! Focus on easier sessions, and run through the hard parks of your solo. Try a schedule like this:

15 min. Scales

10 min. Etudes

20 min. Solo

One Day Before

Todays the day to make sure you're rested and ready! Drink a lot of water today, and just run through your solo once or twice.

15 min. Solo

Most importantly, make sure youre havig fun! Solo's are a great way to show off your hard work, and the more you put into them the better you'll sound! Good luck!
